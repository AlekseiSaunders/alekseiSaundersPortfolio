International Primate Health & Welfare Group

A portal to the papers and projects of the IPH&WG

The International Primate Health & Welfare Group is a group of health and welfare professionals that work towards understanding best practices involved in the care, reproductive health, and wellness of non-human primates.

A member of the IPH&WG reached out to me to help them develop an online resource for the group. Goals for the site are:

  1. Promote the projects and papers of group members
  2. Make resources available to others, professional or otherwise, who might benefit from the collective group knowledge
  3. Act as a fundraising platform to support current or future work by the group
  4. Acknowledge donors who support the group and attract others who might have an interests in support

Technology Used

Based on the goals and needs of the IPH&WG I decided to develop a website based on a template usable with the Ghost.io CMS/CRM platform.

Ghost meets the needs for a fairly easy content development platform. It will allow for the posting of project updates and stories. Integrating posts with photo and video elements is also straightforward.

The end users at IPH&GW are not programmers nor are they developers, I sought out a resource providing an intuitive interface for updating the site and reaching current and potential members through email newsletters

On the fundraising end, Ghost allows for membership levels, something that the group will be able to leverage down the road as the comfort with the platform grows and their network expands.

The Ghost platform is also open source, built using Handlebars, and has a REST API for exporting content. Down the road, if the IPH&WG's needs grow beyond their current template, further customization is possible

Lessons Learned

Drilling down into the needs of a client, versus the wants of a client, can be time consuming - but it is one of the most important parts in web development to do well. As a designer/developer it is my job to steward the client through the confusing options and shiny functionality they may or may not need.

Once again, WYSIWYG editors, no matter how advanced the template they are built upon, are limiting.

A large amount of time will go into making the navigation of the template editor and content creator intuitive to casual users. While this is not the most interesting part of web development it will make my experience of the platform solid and improve my communication skills.

One of the best ways to understand something is to be forced to explain it to professionals outside the working scope of the project.

Site Development

The International Primate Health & Welfare Group website is still under development. We are continuing to discuss project goals and end-user needs. As their desires become more solidified, I will be drafting a full proposal, followed by a contract for site development, deployment, and 1-year maintenance.

Key needs for the group are:

  1. A site that allows members to post project updates and results.
  2. A resource for published papers.
  3. A pathway for attracting and monetizing donors and sponsors
  4. A straightforward explanation of the site's interface to allow member access to writing sections while still limiting posting abilities to group editors.

Technology Used

Providing a CRM/CMS for non-coding users to create blog-like posts and curate information. Client is being introduced to template usage on the Ghost.io framework.
